Assignment 4 Persuasive or Deceptive Visualization?

Matthew Mitchell — mam084@ucsd.edu

Proposition: The energy sector is not the primary driver of national emissions growth.

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Logarithmic y-axis deemphasizes absolute growth and stabilizes year-to-year variation, making energy look consistently small.
  • Neutral palette (gray vs. orange) frames energy as a “subset” of a larger total rather than a focal problem.
  • Conservative spacing and grid reinforce a steady, orderly narrative rather than change or volatility.

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Tight y-range (no baseline padding) magnifies vertical differences across years within each sector.
  • Consistent ordering of sectors and grouped bars makes upward trends easy to scan horizontally.
  • Wider canvas increases separation of groups, helping growth bars stand out against earlier years.

Final Reflection

The two visuals tell conflicting stories with the same underlying data. The first figure uses a log scale and subdued styling to convey that energy is a modest contributor relative to totals, while the second figure uses tighter bounds and layout to highlight sectoral growth—especially in energy and manufacturing. This demonstrates how scaling and framing choices can push an audience toward different conclusions without changing the numbers.
